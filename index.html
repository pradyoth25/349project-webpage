Our Task

The primary goal of this project is to use past purchase and browsing history in order to predict which coupons a customer would buy in a given period of time. Our secondary goal is to perform an exploratory data analysis on the transactional data for 22,873 users on Ponpare, which is a leading Japanese coupon site. We are building a Recommender System, which is analogous to Collaborative Filtering. Recommender systems are very common today, especially for online shopping and video streaming services. Websites with the best recommendations will have the most customer satisfaction and retention.

Approaches

Before trying out any approach, we performed an exploratory analysis on our dataset and reported some findings in a graphical manner. We then tried out different approaches in order to check which algorithm provides the best result. We used a Nearest Neighbor, Decision Trees and Naive Bayes approaches to predict the popularity of coupons based on some attributes like dates available, genre, price and discount. In order to predict the most popular coupons, we took the coupon attributes as inputs and checked each coupon in the test set with the past purchases of the users to give a final prediction.
